Remember — getting stuck is part of the science!
In real life, carbon atoms can remain trapped in a single reservoir for hundreds, thousands, or even millions of years. If you spent most or all of your 15 turns in one place, that is an accurate reflection of how carbon actually behaves in deep ocean sediments, dissolved deep water, or other long-term sinks.
1.Where did your carbon atom spend the most time?
2.Was there anywhere that you got stuck more than one turn?
3.Were you able to get out before turn 15?
4.Think about the places where you were stuck. These are carbon sinks — places where carbon is stored for long periods of time. Why do you think you were stuck in that location?
Hint: think about how carbon is used for things like cellular respiration, in food, photosynthesis.
